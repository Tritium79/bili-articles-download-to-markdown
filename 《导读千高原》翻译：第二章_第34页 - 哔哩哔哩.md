**1914: One or Several Wolves?**

1914年：一匹或几匹狼？ 

**(P34)**

Freud turned the Wolf-Man into a tree. How can we make him into a rhizome and thus create something new? This is the task that Deleuze and Guattari set themselves in the second plateau, which is dated the year that Freud declares the Wolf-Man cured, and they complete this task through the development of the concept of "multiplicity." After seeking treatment for crippling depression and a family history that included the suicides of his sister and father, the Wolf-Man arrived in Vienna seeking Freud's help. The majority of Freud's analysis focused on a dream from the Wolf-Man's childhood: 

弗洛伊德将狼人变成了一棵树。 

那要如何将他转变为根茎并创造新东西呢？ 

所谓“爆改弗洛伊德”计划（有端联想）。 

这即是德勒兹和加塔利在这第二座高原中设定的目标，因此这一章所标的年份对应于弗洛伊德宣称治愈狼人的时间点，而他们达成其目的的方法在于发展“多元性”的概念。 

我认为将“multiplicity”同时译为“多元性”和“多元体”其实并不成问题，甚至可以翻译为“多元者”。 

狼人患有严重的抑郁症并有一段包含其姐姐和父亲自杀的家庭史，他到维也纳向弗洛伊德寻求治疗。 

弗洛伊德把分析的重点放在了狼人童年的一个梦上面： 

_I dreamt that it was night and that I was lying in my bed. (My bed stood with its foot towards the window; in front of the window there was a row of old walnut trees. I know it was winter when I had the dream, and night-time.) Suddenly the window opened of its own accord, and I was terrified to see that some white wolves were sitting on the big walnut tree in front of the window. There were six or seven of them. The wolves were quite white, and looked more like foxes or sheep-dogs, for they had big tails like foxes and they had their ears pricked like dogs when they pay attention to something. In great terror, evidently of being eaten up by the wolves, I screamed and woke up. My nurse hurried to my bed to see what had happened to me. It took quite a long while before I was convinced that it had only been a dream; I had had such a clear and life-like picture of the window opening and the wolves sitting on the tree. At last I grew quieter, felt as though I had escaped from some danger, and went to sleep again._

_我梦见那是在夜里而我正躺在床上。_

_（我的床面向窗户；窗外有一排年代久远的核桃树。我知道做这个梦时是在一个冬天的夜里。）_

_突然窗户自己打开了，我惊恐地发现窗外的核桃树上站着几匹白色的狼。_

_可能有六或七匹狼。_

_这些狼非常白，看上去更像是狐狸或者牧羊犬，因为它们的大尾巴像狐狸而耳朵像狗一样在看东西时会竖起来。_

_由于看上去很明显我会被狼吃掉，巨大的恐惧吓得我尖叫着醒了过来。_

_我的看护人跑到窗前查看发生了什么。_

_我花了很长时间才相信那只是一场梦；但窗外的狼的画面就像真实的现实世界一样。_

_最后我冷静了下来，认为已经脱离了危险，就又睡下了。_

Freud is adamant about dealing with every detail of the dream. He writes in a footnote, "It is always a strict law of dream-interpretation that an explanation that be found for every detail." The detail that's most troubling for Freud is the number of wolves. Everything would have been so much simpler for his analysis if there had only been one wolf. So, Freud tries very hard to reduce the number of wolves. The Wolf-Man's sister used to scare him with a picture of a wolf from a fairy tale, "The Seven Little Goats." Ah, here we go - there is really only one wolf, but in the dream logic the number seven got transposed from the goats to the wolf. It also turns out that the Wolf-Man had a Latin teacher named Wolf who terrified him. Now we're getting somewhere. Because, of course, for Freud the wolf is the father, and the reason that the Wolf-Man fears his father is that he fears the father will castrate him. 

弗洛伊德坚持要处理梦中出现的所有细节。 

那万一患者记忆缺失或者有误不就走远了？ 

他在一处脚注中写道：“对于梦的阐述来说，一套严格的规则要求每个细节都要得到解释。” 

体系化，合理化。 

令弗洛伊德感到最棘手的细节是狼的数量。 

如果只出现了一匹狼，那所有细节都变得容易分析了。 

所以他努力减少狼的数量。 

狼人的姐姐曾用一幅关于童话故事“七只小羊”的图画吓唬过他。 

好，按照这个思路的话——其实只存在一匹狼，是梦的机制将羊的数量转置（transpose）成了狼的数量。 

于是真相是狼人有一个叫沃尔夫（Wolf）的拉丁语老师，然后这个人吓唬过他。 

现在我们得到了一些结论。 

当然，这是因为对弗洛伊德来说狼就指代父亲，而狼人害怕他父亲的原因是他害怕父亲会阉割（castrate）他。
